India is one of the mega under developed country of the world with respect to population status and dynamics.The growing rate of population is a direct factor responsible for enhancement of municipal solid waste (MSW) generation.The huge population of India is responsible for mammoth generation of MSW with daily rate of generation is 400g per capita. The constituents of MSW mainly comprise of compostable items (51-53%), followed by recyclable (17-18%) and having a moisture content of 46.76 %. The MSW generated contains C/N ratio approximately 32 with calorific value of about 1700-1800 Kcal/Kg.Only fraction (10-12%) of the total compostable waste was found to be used for composting. There are plenty of technologies available for generating energy and other useful substances from the MSW ecofriendly, but 60%-90% of MSW generated in cities and towns are directly disposed off on or into land in an unsatisfactory manner.
